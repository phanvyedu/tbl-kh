My story

I have been teaching English in Vietnam for over fifteen years. For most of that time, I believed I understood learning — because I was good at teaching. It took doctoral research to show me how much I didn't know.

The gap between what classroom intuition tells us and what learning science actually shows is wider than most educators realise. I started The Becoming Learners because I wanted to close that gap — for myself first, and then for anyone who cares about learning seriously.

I write from three positions simultaneously: as a teacher who has seen thousands of students struggle and succeed, as a researcher reading the literature carefully, and as a learner who is still figuring it out. I don't think any of those positions can be separated from the others.

I am particularly interested in what happens to learning in the age of AI — not as a technology story, but as a cognitive and human one. And I write from Vietnam deliberately, because the Vietnamese classroom changes the questions in ways that matter.

Why this channel is different

Most education content comes from one angle. TBL sits at the intersection of three.

01
Learning science knowledge
Primary literature — Sweller, Krashen, Bjork, Roediger — not secondhand summaries. Evidence-based, not trend-based.
02
Vietnamese classroom reality
15+ years of real classrooms in Vietnam. Confucian inheritance, EFL context, real students — not hypothetical ones.
03
Doctoral research perspective
Currently in a doctoral program in education. Reading the field seriously, and being honest about what I don't yet know.

Editorial principles

What I commit to in every article and video.

01
Ground every claim in evidence
Every theoretical claim links to primary research. I cite the original paper, not a blog post about the paper.
02
Resist both AI hype and AI panic
The LAI series exists because most AI-in-education discourse is either naive enthusiasm or defensive fear. Neither is useful thinking.
03
Write honestly about uncertainty
The BAL series exists because I believe the most useful educators are the ones who admit what they're still learning.
04
Keep the Vietnamese classroom visible
Most learning science content assumes a Western educational context. I write from Vietnam deliberately — it changes the questions.
