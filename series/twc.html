John Dewey and the classroom that never arrived in Vietnam
Dewey argued that learning is experience — that education lives in the encounter between a mind and a real problem. A century later, his most radical idea is still waiting at the door of the Vietnamese classroom.

Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development — and what AI does to it
Vygotsky gave us the ZPD — the gap between what a learner can do alone and what they can do with guidance. AI can enter that gap. But entering it is not the same as opening it — and the difference matters enormously.
